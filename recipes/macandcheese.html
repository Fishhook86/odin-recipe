Mac and Cheese

Description

Macaroni and cheese, also known as mac and cheese, is a dish of pasta and cheese sauce that's commonly made with macaroni and cheddar cheese. It can be baked in a casserole or cooked on the stovetop, and is often served as a main dish or side dish. The dish is rich and creamy, and is known for its comforting texture and cheesy flavor.

  • Dried Macaroni
  • Egg
  • Salted Butter
  • All Purpose Flour
  • Whole Milk
  • Heaping TSP. dry mustard, plus more to taste
  • Pound of cheese, grated
  • Kosher Salt
  • Seasoned Salt
  • Ground Black Pepper
  1. Cook the macaroni until very firm. The macaroni should be too firm to eat right out of the pot. Drain.
  2. Beat the egg in a small bowl.
  3. Melt the butter in a large pot and sprinkle in the flour. Whisk together over medium-low heat. Cook the mixture for 5 minutes, whisking constantly. Don't let it burn. Pour in the milk, add the mustard, and whisk until smooth. Cook for 5 minutes until very thick. Reduce the heat to low.
  4. Take 1/4 cup of the sauce and slowly pour it into the beaten egg, whisking constantly to avoid cooking the egg. Whisk together till smooth.
  5. Pour the egg mixture into the sauce, whisking constantly. Stir until smooth. Add in the cheese and stir to melt. Add the salt, seasoned salt, pepper, and other optional seasonings. Taste the sauce, adding more salt and seasoned salt as needed. (Do not under-salt!) Pour in the drained, cooked macaroni and stir to combine. Pour into a buttered baking dish, top with extra cheese, and bake at 350Â°F for 20 to 25 minutes, then serve.
